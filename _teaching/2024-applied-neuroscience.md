Fall 2020 - Spring 2024

Psychiatry & Behavioral Sciences - "Applied Positive Neuroscience / F20-S24 / PSYCTRY 79

<blockquote id="apn">Through a nueroscientific and cognitive lens, students are taught how to apply the scientific method to real-world studies. This includes designing experiments around hypothesises, identifying confounding effects, understanding limitations based on statistical analysis, and writing publication-ready papers.
<br><br>
Topics spanned intrapersonal, interpersonal, and extrapersonal contributions to well-being, and how activity and chemistry of key brain regions contribute to each, e.g. influences of mindfulness on prefrontal cortex activity, or how oxytocin system is altered by social interaction. Students learn to recognize relationship between cognitive, social, and emotional competence for healthy development, and how to apply it to their own lives. Through neuroscientific context, introduction to multidisciplinary perspectives on variety of topics that are widely considered significant maturational tasks for young adults, including emotion regulation, managing social relationships, enhancing productivity, and identity development.</blockquote>
